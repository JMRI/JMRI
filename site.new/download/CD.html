JMRI: Installing from CD

Instead of downloading and installing JMRI, you can install it from a CD. This will save you the download time, which can be quite long if you don't have a high-speed connection.

Getting a CD

Because JMRI is open source, there is no restriction on people burning and passing out CDs. This means there are lots of approaches to getting a CD.

Perhaps one of your friends has one, and can make a copy. Or a friend with a high-speed connection might be willing to download the files needed for your computer and put them on a CD.

Some DCC dealers will include a JMRI CD with an order, or will sell them separately. Making a connection with a dealer is highly recommended, because we all benefit when the DCC economy stays healthy. Two dealers who are currently carrying JMRI CDs are:

  • Litchfield Station
  • John's Hobbies

You can also get a CD directly from the JMRI project. Email us at jmri@pacbell.net, and include your computer type and what kind of DCC hardware you use. These CDs contain:

  • The current JMRI production and most recent test versions,
  • Other files needed for installation, including the relevant Java distributions and other files,
  • A copy of our web site and online manual for offline browsing,
  • And a copy of our current source code and build utilities, in case we can interest you in helping improve JMRI.
We charge $5 for the CD, including first-class postage within the U.S. The cost for sending a CD internationally varies; contact us via email for details. PayPal is preferred, but we can work something else out if needed.

CDs are also available in Canada, Austraila, and the U.K., with the advantage of being in local currency. Contact us at jmri@pacbell.net.

Doing the Installation

After inserting the CD into your computer and opening it, you'll find that it contains directories for each of the computer types supported by JMRI: Windows, Linux, MacOS X, and Macintosh.

Open the one that corresponds to your computer type (someday, we might even have an automatic installer to do this).

In that directory, you'll find an "INSTALL.html" file that corresponds to our platform-specific "install guide" file. Open that with a web browser (on many computers, you just have to double click on it).

Now, just follow the instructions, using the individual files you'll find in the directory you've opened.

If anything goes wrong, you can open the "Debugging.html" file at the top level of the CD, or refer back to our web pages.
