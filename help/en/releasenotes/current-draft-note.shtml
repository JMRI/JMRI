Warrants

  • Fixed loss of control when train loses occupation detection (dirty track or pickup, unreliable sensor, etc). Trains could run away at their current speed setting. Speed is set to zero upon loss of detection.
  • Calculation of ramp times and distances improved. However, good data of path lengths and train speed profiles is needed.
  • Fixed two places where a freeze of the Warrant List Table could possibly occur.
  • The address column of the Warrant List table is changed to show a roster id instead of the DCC address. Un-rostered addresses may still be entered and the warrants run with such engines. In this case the column entry is the address bracketed with $ signs.
  • Warrant activity can be traced on the console. An Enable/Disable button in Warrant Preferences activates this feature. Default is tracing disabled.
  • "Slow Speed Assistance" can be applied when a warranted train is inordinately late in getting to the next block of the script. This done only when train speeds are less than 10% throttle approaching a block. If the train has not reached the block within 10 seconds of its scheduled script time, train speed is increased by one "Ramp Step Throttle Increment". This is done at 10 second intervals until either the train reaches the block or has 10% throttle speed. Upon entering the block the original speed is restored.
    This feature will allow balky engines with poor low speed characteristics to reach their destinations without having to use extreme percentages for the "Ramp Step Throttle Increment". The throttle setting for Slow Speed Assistance may be changed in Warrant Preferences. Slow Speed Assistance can be disabled by setting 0%.
